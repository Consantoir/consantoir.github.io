# Top Sirloin Steak with Garlic Balsamic Pan Sauce

[!badge text="Serves 2–4" variant="info"] [!badge text="~30 min + marinate" variant="warning"]

A complete guide — marinade, pan sauce, and perfect searing technique — for a top sirloin that punches way above its price point.

---

## Ingredients

+++ Garlic Herb Balsamic Marinade *(for 1 lb top sirloin)*
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh rosemary, chopped *(or ½ tsp dried)*
- ½ tsp dried thyme
- ¼ tsp black pepper
- ¼ tsp kosher salt *(optional)*
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp honey
+++ Garlic Balsamic Pan Sauce with Mushrooms
- 1 tbsp butter *(plus 1 tbsp to finish)*
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
- ½ cup beef broth or red wine
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
- ¼ cup cremini or button mushrooms, sliced
- ¼ tsp fresh or dried rosemary *(optional)*
- Salt and pepper to taste
+++ For Searing
- 1 tbsp neutral oil *(canola, avocado, etc.)*
- Cast iron or heavy-bottomed skillet
- Meat thermometer
+++

---

## Marinade Instructions

1. Mix all marinade ingredients in a bowl or zip-top bag.
2. Add steak, ensuring it's fully coated.
3. Marinate at least **1 hour** — 4–6 hours preferred, max 12 hours.
4. Remove steak, pat completely dry, and bring to room temperature 20–30 minutes before cooking.

---

## Pan-Searing the Steak

1. Heat pan over medium-high until smoking hot, about **3–5 minutes**.
2. Add neutral oil. Lay steak flat in the pan (away from you). **Don't move it** for 2–3 minutes.
3. Flip and sear the other side another 2–3 minutes.
4. Lower heat to medium. Add 1 tbsp butter, garlic cloves, and rosemary or thyme if using.
5. Baste the steak with the melted butter for 1–2 minutes.
6. Cook to your desired temperature:

| Doneness | Internal Temp |
|---|---|
| Rare | 120–125°F |
| Medium-rare | 130–135°F |
| Medium | 140–145°F |

7. **Rest the steak** 5–10 minutes before slicing against the grain.

---

## Pan Sauce Instructions

Make the sauce in the same skillet right after removing the steak:

1. Reduce heat to medium. Add 1 tbsp butter and mushrooms. Sauté 2–3 minutes.
2. Add garlic and sauté 30 seconds more.
3. **Deglaze** — add balsamic vinegar and broth or wine, whisk well, scraping up all the browned bits from the pan.
4. Stir in Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and rosemary. Simmer 3–4 minutes until slightly reduced.
5. Off heat, whisk in the remaining 1 tbsp butter to finish and gloss the sauce.
6. Season with salt and pepper.

---

!!!tip The dry pat matters
Removing all surface moisture before searing is the single biggest factor in getting a deep, brown crust rather than a gray, steamed exterior. Moisture is the enemy of the Maillard reaction.
!!!

!!!info Steak selection
Top sirloin is lean and affordable. For best results, choose steaks at least ¾ inch thick — thinner steaks cook too fast to develop a proper crust without overcooking the center.
!!!
