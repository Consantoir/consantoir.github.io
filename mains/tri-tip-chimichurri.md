# Tri-Tip with Chimichurri

[!badge text="Serves 4–6" variant="info"] [!badge text="~45 min + rest" variant="warning"]

A bold, smoky spice-rubbed tri-tip paired with a bright, garlicky cilantro chimichurri. Grill or sear — either way this is a showstopper.

---

## Ingredients

+++ Spice Rub
- 2 tbsp sesame seeds
- 1 tbsp smoked paprika
- 2 tsp kosher salt
- 2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
+++ Chimichurri
- 1 cup fresh cilantro, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, finely diced
- 4 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp agave nectar
- Kosher salt to taste
+++ Steak
- 2 lbs tri-tip steak *(or 1–2 lb sirloin steaks)*
+++

---

## Instructions

1. **Make the rub** — mix sesame seeds, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper in a small bowl until combined.
2. **Season the steak** — rub the spice mixture all over the steak. Let rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
3. **Make the chimichurri** — while the steak rests, whisk together cilantro, garlic, red wine vinegar, olive oil, and agave nectar in a bowl. Season with salt to taste. Set aside — it gets better as it sits.
4. **Grill** — prepare a grill for medium-high heat (for charcoal: leave one side off; for gas: turn one burner off). Grill the steak over direct heat, turning occasionally, until a meat thermometer inserted in the thickest part reads:
   - Rare: **125°F**
   - Medium-rare: **130–135°F**
   - Medium: **140–145°F**
5. **Rest** — transfer steak to a cutting board and let rest at least 10 minutes before slicing.
6. **Slice and serve** — cut against the grain into thin slices. Spoon chimichurri generously over the top and pass the rest at the table.

---

!!!tip Against the grain
Tri-tip has changing grain direction — look carefully before slicing. Cutting against the grain makes it dramatically more tender.
!!!

!!!info No grill? No problem.
Sear in a very hot cast iron skillet 3–4 minutes per side, then finish in a 400°F oven until the desired temperature is reached. Rest before slicing.
!!!
