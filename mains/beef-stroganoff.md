# Chef John's Classic Beef Stroganoff

[!badge text="Serves 8" variant="info"] [!badge text="~1 hr 45 min" variant="warning"]

A fairly lean version of the classic — just enough sauce to coat every noodle. Call for more cream if it suits your taste. Satisfying and deeply savory.

*By Chef John.*

---

## Ingredients

+++ Beef
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 2 pounds chuck roast, cut into ½-inch thick strips
- Salt and pepper to taste
+++ Sauce
- 1 tablespoon butter
- ½ medium onion, sliced or diced
- 8 ounces cremini mushrooms, sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- ½ cup white wine, divided
- 2 cups beef broth
- ¼ cup crème fraîche *(see note)*
+++ To Serve
- Buttered egg noodles
- 1 tablespoon fresh chopped chives
+++

---

## Instructions

1. **Season** the beef strips generously with salt and pepper on all sides.
2. **Sear the beef** — heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over high heat until nearly smoking. Add the beef strips and cook, stirring constantly, for about 7 minutes until the liquid evaporates and the meat is browned. Remove beef from the pan and set aside.
3. **Cook the vegetables** — reduce heat to medium. Add butter, mushrooms, and onion to the same pan. Cook and stir until the vegetables are lightly browned, about 7 minutes. Add garlic and cook another 1–2 minutes.
4. **Make the roux** — sprinkle flour over the vegetables and stir to coat. Cook for 1–2 minutes.
5. **Build the sauce** — stir in half the white wine, scraping the bottom of the pan to release any browned bits. Bring to a simmer and cook until slightly thickened, about 3–4 minutes. Add the remaining wine and the beef broth. Stir to combine, bring to a simmer, cover, and cook for about 1 hour until the beef is tender and the sauce has thickened.
6. **Finish** — return beef to the pan and stir in the crème fraîche. Cook 2–3 more minutes. Stir in chives and adjust salt and pepper to taste.
7. **Serve** over buttered egg noodles.

---

!!!tip Crème fraîche substitute
If crème fraîche isn't available, sour cream works perfectly as a 1:1 substitute. Stir it in off the heat to prevent curdling.
!!!

!!!info Low and slow
The long simmer in Step 5 is what makes chuck roast tender and turns the sauce silky. Don't rush it — 60 minutes of gentle simmering is the move.
!!!
