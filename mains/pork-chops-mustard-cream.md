# Pork Chops with Mustard Cream Sauce

[!badge text="Serves 4" variant="info"] [!badge text="~30 min" variant="warning"]

Diner staple done right — pan-seared pork chops finished in the oven and draped in a rich, tangy mustard cream sauce. Turns everyday pork chops into something special.

*Adapted from Food & Wine.*

---

## Ingredients

+++ Pork
- 4 (10-oz) bone-in pork chops, about 1 inch thick
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided
+++ Mustard Cream Sauce
- 1 shallot, finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar *(or sherry vinegar)*
- ½ cup veal stock or low-sodium chicken broth
- ½ cup heavy cream
- 1½ teaspoons Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped flat-leaf parsley
+++

---

## Instructions

1. **Preheat** the oven to 400°F.
2. **Season** the pork chops generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
3. **Sear** — heat 2 tablespoons of the olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet over high heat until almost smoking. Add the pork chops and cook, turning once, until well-browned on both sides, about 4 minutes per side. Transfer the skillet to the oven and roast until cooked through, about 10 minutes (internal temp: 145°F). Transfer chops to a plate and let rest.
4. **Build the sauce** — pour off any excess fat from the skillet. Heat the remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil over medium heat. Add the shallot and cook, stirring, for 2 minutes. Add the vinegar and cook until nearly evaporated, about 1 minute. Pour in the stock and cream and bring to a boil. Cook until the sauce is reduced by about half and lightly coats a spoon, about 4 minutes.
5. **Finish** — stir in the Dijon mustard and parsley. Season with salt and pepper. Return the pork chops and any accumulated juices to the sauce.
6. **Serve** — plate the chops and spoon the mustard cream sauce generously over the top.

---

!!!tip Don't skip the sear
A proper hard sear in a very hot pan builds the fond (browned bits) that makes the pan sauce sing. Don't move the chops until they release naturally.
!!!

!!!info Sauce variations
A tablespoon of whole-grain mustard stirred in at the end alongside the Dijon adds a nice pop of texture and a slightly milder heat.
!!!
