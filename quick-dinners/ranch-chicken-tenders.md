# Crispy Ranch Chicken Tenders

[!badge text="Serves 4" variant="info"] [!badge text="~30 min" variant="warning"] [!badge text="Make Ahead & Freeze" variant="success"]

Baked chicken tenders with a crunchy panko crust and punchy ranch flavor. Diabetic-friendly, Kosher, and low-fat — without skimping on crunch.

*By Tracy.*

---

## Ingredients

+++ Protein
- 1 lb boneless chicken tenderloins
+++ Batter
- 1 tablespoon mayonnaise
- 3 tablespoons Homemade Ranch Dressing Mix *(1 oz packet)*
- 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- ¼ teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- ⅛ teaspoon fresh ground pepper
+++ Breading
- 1¼ cup Panko bread crumbs
+++

---

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to **400°F**. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. Toast the Panko bread crumbs on a baking sheet with a quick spray of cooking spray for 5–7 minutes until lightly golden. Set aside.
3. In a medium-sized bowl, combine all batter ingredients — mayonnaise, ranch dressing mix, Dijon mustard, paprika, salt, and pepper — and mix well.
4. Add chicken tenderloins to the bowl and coat well.
5. Remove chicken from the bowl one at a time and add to the dish of toasted Panko. Keep a parchment paper sheet on the baking sheet for easy cleanup.
6. Using tongs, dredge each chicken tender through bread crumbs until fully coated. Gently press on bread crumbs to coat. Transfer fully-coated tenders to the parchment-lined baking sheet.
7. Return chicken to oven and bake for **15 minutes**.
8. Serve with your favorite homemade condiment, or freeze for later.

---

!!!tip Make ahead and freeze
Let tenders cool completely, then store in the freezer for up to 6 months. When ready to use, place on a parchment-lined baking sheet and bake at 425°F for 20–25 minutes until internal temperature reaches at least 165°F.
!!!

!!!info Ranch dressing mix note
3 tablespoons = 1 oz, which is equivalent to one packet.
!!!
