# Esquites (Mexican Street Corn Salad)

[!badge text="Serves 4–6" variant="info"] [!badge text="Vegetarian" variant="success"] [!badge text="~30 min" variant="warning"]

The off-the-cob version of elotes — all the flavor, much easier to eat.

---

## Ingredients

+++ Corn & Base
- 3 cans fire-roasted corn, drained *(fresh preferred when in season — cut from 4–6 ears and char in a cast iron skillet)*
- 2 pinches each salt and black pepper
+++ Dressing
- 3 tbsp mayonnaise, or more to taste
- ½ tsp ancho chili powder
- ¼ tsp cayenne pepper
- ¼ cup lime juice
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 small shallots, minced
- ¼ small jalapeño, seeded and minced
+++ Toppings
- 4 oz Cotija cheese, crumbled
- ½ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
- 2 limes, cut into wedges
+++

---

## Instructions

1. **Char the corn** — heat a large cast iron skillet over high heat until shimmering. Add drained corn in batches, season with salt and pepper, and cook without stirring until lightly charred, about 3 minutes. Stir once and cook 1 more minute. Set aside.
2. **Make the dressing** — in a large bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, ancho chili powder, cayenne, lime juice, garlic, shallots, and jalapeño.
3. **Toss** — stir in the charred corn until well coated. Fold in half the cilantro and half the Cotija.
4. **Taste and adjust** seasoning. Top with remaining cilantro and Cotija. Serve with lime wedges.

---

!!!tip Canned vs. fresh corn
Fire-roasted canned corn is a great shortcut — just drain and pat dry before charring so it crisps instead of steams. Fresh cut corn straight from the cob gives you more sweetness and better char.
!!!
