# Pickled Deviled Eggs with Candied Bacon Shards

[!badge text="Make Ahead" variant="warning"] [!badge text="Party Starter" variant="success"] [!badge text="Serves 12" variant="info"]

Beet-pickled deviled eggs topped with shards of brown sugar candied bacon.
Start the eggs 3 days ahead — the bacon is made day-of.

---

!!!warning Plan ahead
The eggs need 3 days in the brine. Make the candied bacon the day you serve.
!!!

---

==- 🥚 Pickled Eggs — Make 3 Days Ahead

+++ Ingredients
**Pickling Brine**
- 2 cans (15 oz each) sliced beets, drained — reserve the liquid
- 2 cups beet juice *(from the reserved liquid)*
- 2 cups white sugar
- 1½ cups distilled white vinegar
- 20 whole cloves
- 1 tbsp salt
- 1 tsp ground black pepper
- 4 bay leaves

**Eggs**
- 12 large eggs, hard-boiled and peeled

**Deviled Filling**
- ½ cup mayonnaise
- 1 tsp sweet pickle relish
- 1 tsp prepared yellow mustard
+++ Instructions
1. Measure 2 cups of beet juice from the reserved liquid.
2. Combine beet juice, sugar, vinegar, cloves, salt, pepper, and bay leaves in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer until sugar is fully dissolved, about 5 minutes.
3. Place beets and peeled eggs in a large bowl. Pour the hot brine over them. Let cool, then refrigerate for **3 days**.
4. Drain and discard the brine. Cut each egg in half lengthwise and scoop yolks into a bowl.
5. Mix yolks with mayonnaise, relish, and mustard until smooth. Pipe or spoon filling back into each egg white.
+++

===

==- 🥓 Candied Bacon — Make Day Of

+++ Ingredients
- 1 lb thick-cut bacon
- ¾ cup packed brown sugar
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- Ground black pepper to taste
+++ Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Place bacon on a rack set over a foil-lined baking sheet.
2. Whisk together rice vinegar, maple syrup, and black pepper in a small bowl.
3. Bake bacon 10 minutes, flip, and bake another 5 minutes.
4. Brush both sides generously with the vinegar-syrup mixture and return to oven.
5. Continue basting and baking every 5 minutes until the bacon is deep brown and lacquered, about 20 minutes total.
6. Let cool **completely** on the rack — it crisps as it cools.
+++

!!!tip Let it cool fully
The bacon needs to be completely cool and set before you break it, or it'll bend instead of shard.
!!!

===

---

## Assemble

1. Arrange filled deviled eggs on a serving platter.
2. **Break the cooled candied bacon into rough shards** — mix of large and small for texture and height.
3. Press one or two shards upright into the filling of each egg just before serving.

!!!success Top right before serving
Once the bacon hits the eggs it will start to soften. Add the shards at the last minute for maximum crunch.
!!!
