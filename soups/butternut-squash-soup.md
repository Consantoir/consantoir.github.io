# Instant Pot Butternut Squash Soup

[!badge text="Serves 4–8" variant="info"] [!badge text="Vegetarian" variant="success"] [!badge text="45 min" variant="warning"]

Get the flavor of a slow-simmered squash soup in half the time using an Instant Pot.

*Adapted from Food Network Kitchen.*

---

## Equipment

- 6–8 quart Instant Pot (or other pressure cooker)

---

## Ingredients

+++ Soup
- 3 lbs butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cut into 2-inch cubes
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, smashed
- 3 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 tsp kosher salt, plus more to taste
- ½ tsp ground nutmeg
- ¼ tsp cayenne pepper
- ¼ cup heavy cream *(or coconut cream for vegan)*
- Freshly ground black pepper to taste
+++ Toppings *(optional)*
- Sour cream or Greek yogurt
- Drizzle of olive oil
- Small whole or sliced cremini mushrooms, pan-fried until golden
- Toasted pumpkin seeds
+++

---

## Instructions

1. **Sauté** — set the Instant Pot to Sauté. Melt the butter, then add onion and cook until softened, about 3–4 minutes. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
2. **Pressure cook** — add the squash, broth, salt, nutmeg, and cayenne. Seal the lid and cook on High Pressure for 10 minutes. Quick-release when done.
3. **Blend** — use an immersion blender directly in the pot, or carefully transfer to a blender in batches, and blend until completely smooth.
4. **Finish** — stir in the heavy cream. Taste and adjust salt and pepper.
5. **Serve** — ladle into bowls and top with a dollop of sour cream or yogurt, a drizzle of olive oil, and pan-fried mushrooms if using.

---

!!!tip Texture control
For a thinner soup, add more broth a splash at a time after blending. For extra richness, stir in an additional tablespoon of butter just before serving.
!!!

!!!info No Instant Pot?
Make this on the stovetop: sauté aromatics in a large pot, add squash and broth, bring to a boil, then simmer covered for 30 minutes until the squash is completely tender before blending.
!!!
