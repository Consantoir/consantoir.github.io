# Thai Pumpkin Soup with Coconut Milk

[!badge text="Serves 6" variant="info"] [!badge text="Vegan" variant="success"] [!badge text="50 min" variant="warning"]

The ultimate winter warmer. Serve this fragrant soup with a swirl of coconut milk and fresh cilantro for a fantastic spiced vegan pumpkin soup.

---

## Ingredients

+++ Soup
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 onion, coarsely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, grated
- 1 lemongrass stalk, finely chopped or grated
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 1 bunch fresh cilantro *(roots and stalks finely chopped, leaves reserved)*
- 4 cups vegetable stock
- 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk
- 2 pounds butternut pumpkin *(or butternut squash)*, peeled and chopped
+++ Garnish *(optional)*
- Coconut flakes
- Red chili pepper, sliced
+++

---

## Instructions

1. **Prep the cilantro** — remove the roots and stalky ends from the fresh cilantro. Wash the leaves and dry in a salad spinner or paper towel before roughly chopping. Wash the roots and stalks well to remove any dirt clinging to them. Shake off water and finely chop the stalks and roots — these go in the pot.
2. **Build the base** — heat oil in a large saucepan over medium-high. Add the onion and garlic. Cook until the onion becomes translucent.
3. **Add aromatics** — stir in the ginger, lemongrass, and chopped cilantro stalks and roots. Cook until the stalks soften.
4. **Cook the squash** — add the pumpkin and cook 5 minutes. Pour in the vegetable stock and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low and cook for 30 minutes, or until the pumpkin is completely tender. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly.
5. **Blend** — pour the soup into a blender and blend until smooth. Add most of the chopped cilantro leaves, reserving some for garnish. Blend until the soup is speckled with flecks of green.
6. **Finish with coconut milk** — return to the saucepan and stir in the coconut milk. Start with half and add more to taste. Warm gently over low heat — do not boil.
7. **Serve** — ladle into bowls and top with reserved cilantro, coconut flakes, and sliced red chili if using.

---

!!!tip Pumpkin vs. squash
This recipe works beautifully with butternut squash — feel free to substitute. The article originally references butternut pumpkin, which is the same vegetable known by different names in different countries.
!!!

!!!info Make it ahead
This soup keeps well in the refrigerator for up to 4 days and freezes beautifully for up to 3 months. Add the coconut milk after reheating for best texture.
!!!
